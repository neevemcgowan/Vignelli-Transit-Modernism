Principles

Core Design Philosophy & Guidelines

1. Clarity Above All

  • Information must be readable at a distance
  • Accuracy is less important than usability

2. Typography

  • Humanist sans-serifs (Frutiger, Univers)
  • High contrast, large sizes, mixed case for shape recognition

3. Color Systems

  • Colors represent routes, zones, or meaning
  • Not used decoratively

4. Pictograms

  • Universal silhouettes inspired by Otl Aicher
  • Simple geometry, consistent weight, minimal detail

5. Diagrammatic Maps

  • Schematic, not literal, emphasizes connections and routes
  • 45° and 90° line angles

6. System Thinking

  • Every part fits into a larger grid
  • Information hierarchy is strict and predictable

Influence

Global Impact on Modern Wayfinding

Massimo Vignelli

  • Lead designer of the NYC Subway Map (1972)
  • Known for strict grids, clarity, Helvetica, and systematic design
  • Applied modernism to civic environments

Otl Aicher

  • Designed Munich Olympics pictograms (1972)
  • Created the modern blueprint for universal signage
  • Inspired worldwide transportation iconography

Lance Wyman

  • Mexico City Metro identity system (1968)
  • Used symbols to communicate across languages

Jock Kinneir & Margaret Calvert

  • Designed UK Road Sign System (1957-1967)
  • Established rules still used today: hierarchy, type size, clarity, contrast
