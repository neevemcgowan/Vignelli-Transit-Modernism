# Timeline of Transit Modernism

**1931 — Harry Beck (London Underground Map)**
- Introduces schematic mapping.
- Prioritizes clarity over geography.

**1950s–1960s — Swiss Style Influence**
- Grid systems, universal typography, objective design.
- Helvetica and Univers become standards.

**1964 — Vignelli, Unimark International**
- Begins work on American transit systems.
- Push toward systematic civic communication.

**1972 — Massimo Vignelli (NYC Subway Map)**
- Full schematic redesign.
- Eliminates geographical distortion concerns.

**1972 — Otl Aicher (Munich Olympics)**
- Pictograms become global design standard.
- Universal language in symbols.

**1970s–1980s — Expansion Across Metro Systems**
- Washington Metro, Mexico City Metro, European rail systems adopt modernist rules.
